About:



The Siamese cat is one of the first distinctly recognised breeds of Asian cat. Derived from the Wichianmat landrace, one of several varieties of cats native to Thailand, the original Siamese became one of the most popular breeds in Europe and North America in the 19th century.

Temperament: Sociable, Intelligent, Affectionate, Sensitive, Vocal, Playful

Origin: Thailand

CFA: standard

Common nicknames: Meezer, Mese

Characteristics: Incredibly intelligent, affectionate and opinionated.

Vaccination:


CRP vaccines and anti-rabies vaccines

Health issue:



Respiratory Distress: Due to their brachycephalic (short-faced) features, Siamese cats may experience respiratory distress, especially in hot or humid conditions.
Amyloidosis: Siamese cats may be more susceptible to a condition called amyloidosis, where abnormal protein deposits affect the liver.
Vocalization: While not a health issue, Siamese cats are known for their vocal nature. They may "talk" more than other breeds, which some owners find endearing but others may find challenging.
Respiratory Issues: Siamese cats may be more susceptible to respiratory infections, including feline herpesvirus and upper respiratory infections (URIs).
Regular veterinary check-ups, a well-balanced diet, proper dental care, and a clean and safe environment are crucial for maintaining the health of Siamese cats.
