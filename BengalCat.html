About:



The Bengal cat is a domesticated cat breed created from a hybrid of the Asian leopard cat, with domestic cats, especially the spotted Egyptian Mau.

Origin: United States

Temperament: Curious, Friendly, Affectionate, Alert, Confident, Dependable

Foundation bloodstock: Egyptian Mau, Abyssinian, and others (domestic); Asian leopard cat (wild)

TICA: standard

Characteristics: Affectionate, playful, and can get along with people of all ages as well as other pets.

Vaccination:


CRP vaccines and anti-rabies vaccines

Health issue:




Patellar Luxation: This is a condition where the kneecap (patella) can dislocate, leading to lameness. It's more common in certain breeds, including Bengals.
Distal Neuropathy: This is a neurological disorder that can affect the hind limbs of Bengals, leading to weakness and difficulty walking. The exact cause is not always clear.
Allergies: Some Bengals may be prone to allergies, which can manifest as skin issues or respiratory problems. Identifying and avoiding allergens is important.
