About:



Birds are a group of warm-blooded vertebrates constituting the class Aves, characterised by feathers, toothless beaked jaws, the laying of hard-shelled eggs, a high metabolic rate, a four-chambered heart, and a strong yet lightweight skeleton.

Scientific name: Aves

Domain: Eukaryota

Kingdom: Animalia

Phylum: Chordata

Health issue:



Feather Plucking: Behavioral issues such as feather plucking or self-mutilation can be a sign of stress, boredom, or underlying health problems.
Beak and Nail Disorders: Overgrown or deformed beaks and nails can occur, often as a result of poor nutrition or genetics.
Liver Disease: Birds, especially those on a high-fat diet or exposed to toxins, may be prone to liver disease. This can affect their overall health and metabolism.
