About:



The Persian cat, also known as the Persian longhair, is a long-haired breed of cat characterised by a round face and short muzzle. The first documented ancestors of Persian cats might have been imported into Italy from Khorasan as early as around 1620, however, this has not been proven.

Health issue:



Respiratory Issues: Due to their brachycephalic (flat-faced) structure, Persian cats may be prone to respiratory issues, such as breathing difficulties and nasal discharge.
Dental Problems: Persian cats may be prone to dental issues, including periodontal disease, due to their brachycephalic facial structure and the potential for misaligned teeth.
Urinary Tract Issues: Persian cats may be prone to urinary tract issues, including bladder stones and infections. Ensuring proper hydration and a balanced diet is essential.
