About:



The Golden Retriever is a Scottish breed of retriever dog of medium size. It is characterised by a gentle and affectionate nature and a striking golden coat. It is commonly kept as a pet and is among the most frequently registered breeds in several Western countries.

Life span: 10 – 12 years

Temperament: Friendly, Kind, Intelligent, Reliable, Trustworthy, Confident

Weight: Female: 25–32 kg, Male: 30–34 kg

Origin: United Kingdom, Scotland, England

Colors: Dark Golden, Light Golden, Cream, Golden

Height: Female: 51–56 cm, Male: 56–61 cm

The Kennel Club: standard.

Vaccination:

10-12 weeks : DA2PP*

Health issue:



Some common health problems in Golden Retrievers include hip dysplasia, cancer, thyroid, heart issues as well as seizures.

Regular veterinary check-ups, a balanced diet, regular exercise, and preventive care can help maintain the health and well-being of Golden Retrievers. Responsible breeding practices, including health screenings, can also contribute to reducing the risk of hereditary health issues. Additionally, early detection and intervention are crucial for managing and treating health problems in dogs.
