About:


The Labrador Retriever, or simply Labrador, is a British breed of retriever gun dog. It was developed in the United Kingdom from fishing dogs imported from the colony of Newfoundland, and was named after the Labrador region of that colony.


Lifespan: 10 – 12 years (Adult)

Temperament: Friendly, Even Tempered, Kind, Intelligent, Outgoing, Agile, Gentle, Trusting

Weight: Male: 29–36 kg, Female: 25–32 kg

Origin: United Kingdom, Newfoundland

Colors: Black, Chocolate, Yellow

Height: Male: 57–62 cm, Female: 55–60 cm

The Kennel Club (UK): standard.

Vaccination:


6-8 weeks : DA2PP*
10-12 weeks : DA2PP*
14-16 weeks : Rabbies**,DA2PP*
Annual Examination : Rabbies**,DA2PP*

Health issue:



Obesity is a serious health issue in Labradors because it can increase their risk of heart and liver disease, joint problems.

Regular veterinary check-ups, a balanced diet, regular exercise, and preventive care can contribute to the overall well-being of Labradors and help catch and address health issues early on. Responsible breeding practices, including screening for genetic conditions, can also help reduce the risk of hereditary health problems.
