About:



The German Shepherd, also known in Britain as an Alsatian, is a German breed of working dog of medium to large size. The breed was developed by Max von Stephanitz using various traditional German herding dogs from 1899. It was originally bred as a herding dog, for herding sheep.

Life span: 9 – 13 years

Colors: Black, White, Black & Tan, Black & Silver, Sable, Red & Black, Grey

Weight: Male: 30–40 kg, Female: 22–32 kg

Temperament: Stubborn, Curious, Intelligent, Alert, Obedient, Loyal, Protective, Confident, Watchful, Courageous, Brave.

Height: Male: 60–65 cm, Female: 55–60 cm.

Vaccination:

Health issue:



German Shepherds commonly also get gastric dilation and volvulus (GDV), commonly known as bloat, as well as cancer, allergies, heart disease, and a few other conditions.

It's important for German Shepherd owners to be aware of these potential health issues and to provide regular veterinary check-ups, a balanced diet, regular exercise, and preventive care to ensure the well-being of their dogs. Regular veterinary visits can help catch and address health issues early on.
